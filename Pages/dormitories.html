Dormitories

The main concept of Shinawatra University Dormitory is to establish a “Living, Learning and Caring Center” for students to enhance their quality of life, with excellent living facilities, surrounded by a beautiful environment. All rooms are regularly cleaned and security guards are always on duty, to ensure a safe and happy stay.

We offer accommodation to every student studying at Pathum Thani Campus with two five-floor dormitories. The two dorms are a 7 minute walk from the Main Hall and a 5 minute walk to the canteen. Sports facilities can also be found nearby. These include a fitness room located within the Dormitory 2 building, a football field which can be used also for track and field events, a basketball court, and a couple of tennis courts. Table tennis and other indoor sports are either held in the Dormitory 2 or in the sports center. Laundry services are open to student customers at a reasonable charge.

Rooms

Rooms in either dormitory are available at a reasonable rate, with 4 types to choose from. These are single, double, triple and quadruple rooms, which are of 36 and 42 sq. meters in area for standard rooms in Dormitory 1 and Dormitory 2 respectively. All rooms are air-conditioned and completely serviced, with computer network connections, telephone, refrigerator and hot water supply. Central facilities are also provided in each dormitory, with a TV room with wireless network connection, study rooms, pantry and recreational areas, including a karaoke room to make the students’ life more comfortable and enjoyable.

Dormitory Services

Some useful information:

A bedspread, blanket, pillowcase and sheets are provided in every room. Maids will clean your room twice a week. However, please help by keeping your room clean and tidy all the time. The Dormitory management will be responsible for the laundry of the bedspread, blanket, pillowcase and sheets. One TV room with a pantry is situated on each floor. Hot and cold drinking water is available on each floor. Toilet paper is supplied to each room.


Safety and security systems:

Heat detectors and a wet-pipe sprinkler system for fire protection are installed in all rooms. A key card is needed to enter each floor. The entry and exit areas are watched constantly by security personnel, and a closed-circuit television system.

Organized Functions

Under the concept “Living, Learning and Caring Center”, the Dormitory places great importance on the happiness, convenience, and comfort of residents. In this regard, the following functions and services are organized regularly or from time to time. Cultural and international events Sports activities e.g. Basketball, Badminton, Tennis etc Sightseeing trips (occasionally) during weekends A weekend shuttle bus is provided at minimum cost to major shopping malls near Pathum Thani.

Some Important Rules and Regulations

For a large number of people living together, rules and regulations are necessary to avoid conflicts and preserve peace. In this context, residents are to observe the following rules and regulations. Students are forbidden to possess, use or sell illegal drugs. Students are forbidden to drink alcohol or gamble on University property or while on excursions organized by the University. Students are required to be in their rooms before 11 p.m. weekdays, and 12 p.m. Saturdays, Sundays and holidays. Students are required to show their ID cards or write their names at the front gate security kiosk before going out and entering the campus. Students are not allowed to smoke in any building. If any student behavior is deemed detrimental to their personal safety, another student’s safety, or the dormitory’s personnel and property, that student will face disciplinary action. Bullying will not be tolerated under any circumstances, and any person who commits this act will face disciplinary action. Students causing any damage or loss to property shall be liable to pay the cost of repair or replacement in whole or in part, as the case may be. Students must attend classes and related activities, fully and on time. Students must show consideration to their fellow inhabitants and the university community. Students must obey instructions given legitimately by lecturers and the dormitory management. Students who violate the rules and regulations will receive warnings: a verbal warning for the first violation, a written warning (a copy of which will be sent to the parents) if committed a second time, and a final warning for the third time. Students committing a serious violation of rules and regulations shall be penalized accordingly.

Further Guidance

For your own and other people’s convenience and safety, students must follow these guidelines: Do not make any noise after 10:00 p.m. Do not move to another room, or swap rooms with another student without the management’s approval. Do not disturb other residents. Do not leave valuables or cash lying around in your room. Lock them in your desk drawer. Do not act dangerously or cause damage to the dormitory property. Help save energy and water. Please turn off the lights, air-conditioner and water taps when you leave your room. Carry your student ID card and keys at all times. Inform the dormitory or university staff of any difficulties or feel that you are in danger. Male students must not enter the female students’ quarters and vice versa. Pets are not allowed in the dormitory except in certain cases. Seek advice from the Dormitory management on this issue.
